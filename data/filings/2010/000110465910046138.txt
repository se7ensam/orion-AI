<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">Tarak Mehta to join Executive Committee as head of
ABB&#146;s Low Voltage Products</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Mehta to replace Tom Sj&#246;kvist, who
retires in September&nbsp;after 38 years with the company</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Zurich,
Switzerland, Aug.&nbsp;27, 2010 &#151; Tarak Mehta, the global head of ABB&#146;s
Transformers business, has been appointed to ABB&#146;s Executive Committee as head
of the Low Voltage Products division as of Oct.&nbsp;1, 2010. He replaces Tom
Sj&#246;kvist, who is retiring at the end of September.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Mehta,
a US citizen, has been responsible for ABB&#146;s Transformers unit since 2007. It
is the world&#146;s largest transformer business, with 16,500 employees.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;Tarak
has driven growth in the Transformers business organically and through the
integration of several niche acquisitions, developing a very competitive
business with an innovative, market-focused portfolio and a robust service
offering,&#148; said Joe Hogan, ABB&#146;s chief executive officer.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Mehta
joined ABB in 1998 and has held various management positions in the Power
Products division in the US, Sweden and Switzerland. Before joining ABB, he
worked for 8 years at Cooper Power Systems in the US. Mehta has Bachelor&#146;s
degree in Mechanical Engineering from Purdue University and an MBA in Finance
and Marketing from the University of Chicago.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Tom
Sj&#246;kvist is retiring from ABB after 38 years with the company. He began as a
trainee at ASEA in Sweden in 1972, and has since held a wide variety of roles
in Sweden, the US, Germany and Switzerland. He joined the ABB Executive
Committee in 2006 as head of the Automation Products division.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;Under
Tom&#146;s leadership, the business developed a rich portfolio of innovative
technologies. In 2010, Tom became head of the Low Voltage Products division,
which has proven an equally successful business with double-digit growth in its
first half year,&#148; Hogan said.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">ABB
(www.abb.com) is a leader in power and automation technologies that enable
utility and industry customers to improve performance while lowering
environmental impact. The ABB Group of companies operates in around 100 countries
and employs about 117,000 people.</font></p>
