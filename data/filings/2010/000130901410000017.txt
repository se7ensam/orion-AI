<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt"><B>For Immediate Release &#150; January&nbsp;11, 2010</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">VANCOUVER, CANADA&#150; Ballard Power Systems (TSX: BLD; NASDAQ: BLDP) announced that it has received a
sales order for five FC<I>velocity</I><sup>TM</sup>-HD6 power modules from Advanced Public Transportation
Systems bv (APTS), a specialty bus OEM in The Netherlands, part of the VDL Group. The 150-kilowatt
power modules will be installed and integrated by Vossloh-Kiepe with hybrid electric drive systems
in 18-meter (60-foot) articulated Phileas buses manufactured by APTS.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">Phileas is a new concept for passenger transport on high frequency dedicated bus lanes. When
running on a free bus lane and equipped with magnetic markers for electronic lane assistance and
precision docking, Phileas buses can offer the advantages of rapid rail transport.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">Ballard&#146;s power module is a &#145;plug-and-play&#146; fuel cell-based product that enables system integrators
to build clean energy buses more easily and at lower cost. These power modules incorporate
sub-system components addressing humidification, hydrogen pressure regulation, hydrogen
recirculation, water management, ventilation and controls. Since these sub-system components and
fuel cell stacks are optimized within a power module, they can be more readily integrated with the
bus hybrid electric drive system.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">Michael Goldstein, Ballard&#146;s Chief Commercial Officer stated &#147;The deployment of our
FC<I>velocity</I><sup>TM</sup>-HD6 power modules with APTS and other customers such as BC Transit and
Transport for London mean that fuel cell-based power solutions are gaining real traction in the
global mass transit marketplace, delivering GHG reductions and energy conservation benefits&#148;. He
continued, &#147;Ballard&#146;s foundation of reliability and durability are an ideal fit for our customers
in motive power, backup power and distributed generation.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">All the power modules will be shipped to APTS by the second quarter of 2010 and the first APTS
Phileas bus incorporating a Ballard power module will be produced this year. Two Phileas buses
powered by Ballard fuel cell modules will be operating in Cologne, Germany and two will be
operating in Amsterdam. APTS currently has 70 Phileas buses in operation worldwide.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">Ruud Bouwman, Director of APTS said &#147;We are certainly pleased to be working with Ballard on our
Phileas buses. APTS sees a sizable potential European market for this fuel cell &#145;tram on tires&#146;
concept, with each fuel cell bus reducing CO2 emissions up to 155 tons per year.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">Ballard&#146;s participation in demonstration programs through five generations of heavy duty fuel cell
power modules has yielded over two million kilometers (1.2&nbsp;million miles) of actual revenue
service, transporting over seven million passengers on buses in various locations around the world.
Ballard&#146;s next generation heavy duty fuel cell power module, the FC<I>velocity</I><sup>TM</sup>-HD6,
delivers enhanced fuel cell durability and improved efficiency at a reduced cost, while offering an
industry leading 12,000-hour (5-year) warranty.
