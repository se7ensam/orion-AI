<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt">&#160;</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 2.9pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Arial">On 26 November 2009, The Royal Bank of Scotland Group plc (&#8220;RBSG&#8221;) entered into a State Aid Commitment Deed with HM Treasury of the United Kingdom government (&#8220;HM Treasury&#8221;) containing commitments and undertakings given by RBSG to HM Treasury that are designed to ensure that HM Treasury is able to comply with the commitments given by it to the European Commission for the purpose of obtaining approval for the State aid provided to RBSG.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 2.9pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Arial">As part of these commitments, RBSG agreed that RBS Holdings N.V. (&#8220;RBSH NV&#8221;) will not pay investors any coupons on, or exercise any call rights in relation to, the hybrid capital instruments listed in the Schedule hereto (the &#8220;Affected Securities&#8221;), unless in any such case there is a legal obligation to do so, for an effective period of two years. RBSH NV and its group companies are also subject to restrictions on the exercise of call rights in relation to their other hybrid capital instruments.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>
