<DIV align="center" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt"><B>Agria Implements Rotation of Independent Auditor Policy</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt"><B>Beijing, China &#151; December&nbsp;31, 2010 </B>&#151; Agria Corporation (NYSE: GRO) (the &#147;Company&#148; or &#147;Agria&#148;), a
China-based company with investments in the agriculture sector, announced today that it has
implemented its independent auditor rotation policy and changed its independent auditor for the
year ending December&nbsp;31, 2010 from Ernst &#038; Young Hua Ming (&#147;Ernst &#038; Young&#148;) to GHP Howarth. Ernst &#038;
Young audited Agria&#146;s financial statements for each of the previous six years. The change was
approved by Agria&#146;s audit committee and full board of directors. Agria and Ernst &#038; Young did not
have any disagreements on matters of accounting principles or practices, financial statement
disclosure, or auditing scope or procedures that, if not resolved to the satisfaction of Ernst &#038;
Young, would have caused it to make reference to the subject matter of the disagreement in
connection with its report on Agria&#146;s consolidated financial statements.
</DIV>

<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt"><B>About Agria Corporation</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt">Agria Corporation (NYSE: GRO) is a China-based company with investments in the agricultural sector.
For more information about Agria Corporation, please visit www.agriacorp.com.
</DIV>
