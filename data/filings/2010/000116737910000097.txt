&#8211; </font>The Alcon Independent Director Committee (the &#8220;IDC&#8221;) announced today
that Professor Hans Caspar von der Crone, a leading Swiss legal and corporate
governance expert, has concluded that a recommendation by the IDC is an
indispensable first step before the board of Alcon, Inc. (&#8220;Alcon&#8221;) can decide on
the merger proposal of Novartis AG (&#8220;Novartis&#8221;). This conclusion refutes
Novartis&#8217; public implications that it would be able to unilaterally impose the
merger irrespective of the IDC&#8217;s position once Novartis becomes Alcon&#8217;s majority
shareholder.</font></div>

copy of the legal opinion is available on the IDC&#8217;s website:
www.transactioninfo.com/alcon</font></div>
      <div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br></div>
      <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">In his
legal opinion, Professor von der Crone confirmed the IDC&#8217;s rights and
obligations under Swiss law and, in doing so, supported many of the legal
arguments previously put forth by the IDC. In concluding that &#8220;the Alcon board
will not be able to validly decide on Novartis&#8217; merger proposal without the
IDC&#8217;s prior recommendation of that proposal,&#8221; Professor von der Crone reasoned
in particular that:</font></div>

      <div>

      shareholder is clearly conflicted with respect to a merger between the
      company and such majority
shareholder.</font></div>

      will not be legally effective if the counterparty to the agreement was
      aware of the conflict of interests at the board level. Thus, a merger
      agreement between Alcon and Novartis will not be legally effective if it
      is approved by a conflicted Alcon board only, because Novartis, after
      having appointed the conflicted directors to the Alcon board, is fully
      aware of the conflict of interests within the Alcon
  board.</font></div>

      <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">The IDC
commissioned Professor von der Crone, as an independent third party and expert
in Swiss corporate law and corporate governance best practices, to review all
relevant corporate documents and issue a legal opinion as to the internal
approval process that must be followed by Alcon in connection with Novartis&#8217;
merger proposal. Hans Caspar von der Crone is a Professor for Private,
Commercial and Corporate Law at the University of Zurich (Ordinarius). He
graduated from the University of Zurich and from Yale Law School. Professor von
der Crone was Chairman of the Swiss Takeover Board from 1999 through October
2005 and before that he was a member of the Commission of Regulation of the
Swiss Stock Exchange, the supervisory body that preceded the Swiss Takeover
Board. He is also a member of several boards and professional organizations and
a successfully practicing attorney in particular in the fields of Corporate and
Commercial Law, Capital Market and Securities Law.</font></div>

Plaskett, Chairman of the IDC, said, &#8220;Since the beginning of this process, the

continue to hope that we can reach a negotiated deal, Professor von der Crone&#8217;s
legal opinion makes clear that, regardless of Novartis&#8217; ultimate course of
action, the IDC&#8217;s recommendation is a mandatory step prior to the consummation
of Novartis&#8217; merger proposal. The independent directors have a duty to protect
the rights of Alcon and its minority shareholders, and we are committed to
taking all available and appropriate actions to do so.&#8221;</font></div>

directly.&#8221;</font></div>
      <div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br></div>
      <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Information

      <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">The value
of Novartis&#8217; proposal, which was deemed grossly inadequate by the IDC and its
advisors, has steadily deteriorated since it was first announced. As of June 25,
2010, the Novartis proposal of 2.8 Novartis shares for each Alcon share is
valued at $136.78 per Alcon share, highlighting the inferiority of the
consideration offered to the minority shareholders relative to the cash that
Nestl&#233; S.A. is receiving. The current value of Novartis&#8217; merger proposal
represents an approximately 25% discount to the $181.71 in cash (including
interest to be paid to Nestl&#233; S.A., computed through the end of August 2010)
that Novartis has agreed to pay Nestl&#233; S.A. for their 52% control stake in
Alcon. It also represents a 17% and 15% discount to Alcon&#8217;s closing prices one
day and one month before the proposal was announced, respectively. Meanwhile,
Alcon&#8217;s performance remains strong, having exceeded analyst estimates in each of
the past two quarters that it has reported since the merger proposal was first
announced.</font></div>
