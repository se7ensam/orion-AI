<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">ABB resolves Foreign
Corrupt Practices Act issues and will pay a total of $58.3 million</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Zurich,
Switzerland, September&nbsp;30, 2010 &#151; Today, ABB announced settlements of
anti-bribery investigations conducted by the U.S. Department of Justice (&#147;DOJ&#148;)
and the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (&#147;SEC&#148;).&#160; ABB Ltd and ABB Inc. will pay a total of
$58.3 million in disgorgement, prejudgment interest and penalties to the DOJ
and the SEC to resolve charges arising from payments related to projects in
Mexico.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">ABB
Ltd initiated these matters in a voluntary disclosure to the DOJ and SEC
beginning in 2005.&#160; This settlement also
covers the Oil for Food program investigation in which ABB cooperated.&#160; The charges involved ABB Inc.&#146;s Network
Management business unit in Sugar Land, Texas performing contracts in Mexico
from 1997 to 2005 and certain subsidiaries performing contracts under the U.N.
Oil for Food program in Iraq from 2000-2004.&#160;
The payments related to the resolution of these matters will have no
additional impact on ABB&#146;s EBIT and net income.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">ABB
Inc. pled guilty to one count of conspiracy to violate the anti-bribery
provisions of the U.S.&#160; Foreign Corrupt
Practices Act (&#147;FCPA&#148;) and one count of violating the anti-bribery provision of
the FCPA.&#160; ABB Ltd has agreed to a
deferred prosecution agreement, which includes provisions related to the
involvement of a subsidiary in Jordan in the Oil for Food program.&#160; In the related SEC proceeding, ABB Ltd
settled civil charges of FCPA bribery, books and records, and internal controls
violations based on the conduct of the Sugar Land business unit and the conduct
of certain subsidiaries participating in the Oil for Food program.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">ABB
has cooperated fully with the DOJ and SEC and has put in place a global comprehensive
compliance and integrity program the DOJ has said &#147;may become a benchmark for
the industry.&#148;&#160; In lieu of an external
compliance monitor, the DOJ and SEC have agreed to allow ABB to report on its
continuing compliance efforts and the results of the review of its internal
processes for a three-year period going forward.&#160; The DOJ settlement expressly recognizes ABB&#146;s
&#147;extraordinary cooperation&#148; and &#147;extensive investigation.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Diane de Saint Victor, General Counsel ABB said<b>: </b>&#147;We are pleased to announce today that
the DOJ and SEC FCPA investigations of the matters included in our disclosures
since 2005 are behind us.&#160; ABB is
committed to fostering a culture where integrity is woven into the fabric of
everything we do.&#160; We want integrity
embedded in our people, our businesses and processes.&#160; This means acting responsibly as individuals
and as a company.&#160; We regularly evaluate
our culture of integrity, and will continue raising the bar relentlessly.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">ABB
(www.abb.com) is a leader in power and automation technologies that enable
utility and industry customers to improve performance while lowering
environmental impact. The ABB Group of companies operates in around 100
countries and employs about 117,000 people.</font></p>
