Plaskett, Chairman of the IDC, said, &#8220;We look forward to negotiating a deal that affords fair value to Alcon&#8217;s minority shareholders. An agreed transaction is in the best interests of all stakeholders and is clearly preferable to protracted litigation, which would delay critical steps in the integration process. However, we are ready to defend the rights of Alcon and its minority shareholders if Novartis refuses to negotiate a fair deal.&#8221;</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">The closing of the Nestl&#233;/Novartis transaction satisfies the condition of the election of Novartis&#8217; five additional designees to Alcon&#8217;s board, which was made on August 16<font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 70%; VERTICAL-ALIGN: text-top">th</font> despite their overwhelming rejection by minority shareholders. The Novartis designees have a clear conflict of interest with respect to any decision regarding Novartis&#8217; merger proposal to minority shareholders. As previously announced by the IDC and supported in a legal opinion issued by Professor Hans Caspar von der Crone, a leading Swiss corporate governance expert, the role of non-conflicted directors in related-party transactions is established both by Swiss law and Alcon&#8217;s organizational documents. Accordingly, a positive recommendation by the IDC is requir
ed with respect to any related-party transaction between Alcon and Novartis, including the merger proposal, and the IDC&#8217;s powers and duties cannot be altered without the consent of the IDC.</font></div>
