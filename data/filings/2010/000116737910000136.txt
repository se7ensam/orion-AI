<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">RiskMetrics confirmed the IDC&#8217;s view that Novartis&#8217; five proposed directors are &#8220;designees,&#8221; citing Section 2.1(f) of the 2008 Nestl&#233;-Novartis Shareholder Agreement and Article VIII of the Alcon Organizational Regulations, and that therefore they are conflicted and must abstain from voting on any matter involving Novartis. The IDC notes that the term &#8220;designee&#8221; was specifically added to Article VIII of the Alcon Organizational Regulations following the 2008 Nestl&#233;-Novartis transaction.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Thomas G. Plaskett, Chairman of the IDC, said, &#8220;These are strong statements from leading independent corporate governance experts, and they are rightly concerned by Novartis&#8217; attempt to force a grossly inadequate offer on minority shareholders in blatant disregard of their rights to fair process and fair value. We are pleased that both RiskMetrics Group and Glass Lewis recognize the clear conflict of interest facing the Novartis designees with respect to the related-party transactions between Alcon and Novartis, including the Novartis merger proposal and related matters. This view reinforces the IDC&#8217;s longstanding position with respect to their conflicted status and is consistent with established principles of good corporate governance. We note that both RiskMetrics and Glass Lewis recommend that minority shareholders us
e their vote at the extraordinary general meeting to send a message to Novartis that they will not idly stand by in the face of Novartis&#8217; coercive tactics.&#8221;</font></div>

In particular, Manifest stated, &#8220;The presence of a majority of Novartis appointed directors could lead to conflict which might perceivably hinder the unity and operation of the Board.&#8221; Manifest does not make voting recommendations to shareholders.</font></div>
