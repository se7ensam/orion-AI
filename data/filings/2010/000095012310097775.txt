<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>51job, Inc. Appoints New Independent Director and Regains Nasdaq Compliance</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>SHANGHAI, China, October&nbsp;19, 2010 </B>&#150; <B>51job, Inc. (Nasdaq: JOBS)</B>, a leading provider of integrated human resource
services in China, announced today that the board of directors has appointed James Jianzhang Liang as an independent
director and a member of the audit committee.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">James Jianzhang Liang is a co-founder and the chairman of the board of directors of Ctrip.com International, Ltd., a
leading travel service provider of hotel accommodations, airline tickets, packaged tours and corporate travel
management in China. Mr.&nbsp;Liang served as Chief Executive Officer of Ctrip from 2000 to January&nbsp;2006 and has been a
member of Ctrip&#146;s board of directors since inception. Prior to founding Ctrip, Mr.&nbsp;Liang held a number of technical and
managerial positions with Oracle Corporation from 1991 to 1999 in the United States and China, including the head of
the ERP consulting division of Oracle China from 1997 to 1999. He also serves on the board of directors and is an audit
committee member of Home Inns &#038; Hotel Management Inc., a leading economy hotel chain in China. Mr.&nbsp;Liang received his
Bachelor and Master degrees from the Georgia Institute of Technology. He also attended an undergraduate program at
Fudan University.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We are very pleased to add James to our board of directors as he brings a wealth of experience in building and growing
highly successful businesses in China,&#148; said Rick Yan, President and Chief Executive Officer of 51job, Inc.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Pursuant to this appointment, the Company&#146;s board of directors is now comprised of five members, a majority of whom are
independent directors, and its audit committee has three members, all of whom are independent directors. The Company
has returned to full compliance with Nasdaq listing requirements.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>About 51job</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">51job, Inc. (Nasdaq: JOBS) is a leading provider of integrated human resource services in China with a strong focus on
recruitment related services. Through print advertisements in <I>51job Weekly </I>and online recruitment services at
<I>www.51job.com</I>, 51job enables enterprises to attract, identify and recruit employees and connects millions of job
seekers with employment opportunities. 51job also provides a number of other value-added human resource services,
including business process outsourcing, training, executive search and compensation and benefits analysis. 51job has a
call center in Wuhan and a nationwide sales office network spanning 25 cities across China.

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt; text-indent: 4%">David K. Chao has been a director of the Company since 2000. Mr.&nbsp;Chao is a Co-founder and

Ltd. Mr.&nbsp;Chao serves on the boards of directors of numerous DCM portfolio companies. He is a
Management Board member of the Stanford Graduate School of Business Board of Trustees. He also
serves on the board of directors of Legend Capital. Mr.&nbsp;Chao received his Bachelor of Arts degree
in Economics and East Asian Studies (Anthropology) with high honors from Brown University and his
Master of Business Administration degree from Stanford University.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt; text-indent: 4%">Hiroyuki Honda has been a director of the Company since July&nbsp;2006. Mr.&nbsp;Honda is an executive

been a director of Recruit since 2005. Mr.&nbsp;Honda joined Recruit in 1984 and is currently in charge
of the Corporate Planning Office and Business Development Office. Mr.&nbsp;Honda also serves as a board
member of Recruit&#146;s various joint ventures with Toyota Motor Corporation and Yahoo! Japan
Corporation. In over two decades at Recruit, Mr.&nbsp;Honda has acted as General Manager of the

resource related business group. Mr.&nbsp;Honda received his Bachelor of Law degree in Labor Law from
Hokkaido University in 1984.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt; text-indent: 4%">James Jianzhang Liang was appointed a director of the Company in October&nbsp;2010. Mr.&nbsp;Liang is a

travel service provider of hotel accommodations, airline tickets, packaged tours and corporate
travel management in China. He served as Chief Executive Officer of Ctrip from 2000 to January&nbsp;2006
and has been a member of Ctrip&#146;s board of directors since inception. Prior to founding Ctrip, Mr.
Liang held a number of technical and managerial positions with Oracle Corporation from 1991 to 1999
in the United States and China, including the head of the ERP consulting division of Oracle China
from 1997 to 1999. Mr.&nbsp;Liang also serves on the board of directors of Home Inns &#038; Hotel Management
Inc. Mr.&nbsp;Liang received his Bachelor and Master degrees from the Georgia Institute of Technology.
He also attended an undergraduate program at Fudan University.

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt; text-indent: 4%">Donald L. Lucas has been a director of the Company since February&nbsp;2004. In 1960, Mr.&nbsp;Lucas
began a seven-year participation, including acting as both a general partner and a limited partner
with Draper, Gaither &#038; Anderson, the first venture capital firm organized on the West Coast in the
United States. Since 1967, Mr.&nbsp;Lucas has been actively engaged in venture capital activities as a
private individual. Mr.&nbsp;Lucas currently serves as a board member of Cadence Design Systems, Inc.,
DexCom, Inc., Oracle Corporation and Vimicro International Corporation. He also serves as a
director for several privately held companies. Mr.&nbsp;Lucas is the former chairman of the board of the
Stanford Institute for Economic Policy and Research and a Trustee of the University of Santa Clara.
Mr.&nbsp;Lucas received his Bachelor of Arts degree from Stanford University and his Master of Business
Administration degree from the Stanford Graduate School of Business.
