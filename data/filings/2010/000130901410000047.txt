<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt"><B>Ballard Receives Federal Government Funding to Advance Fuel Cell Power Module Technology</B>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">For Immediate Release &#150; January&nbsp;21, 2010


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">VANCOUVER, CANADA&#150; Ballard Power Systems (TSX: BLD; NASDAQ: BLDP) announced that it has been
awarded up to $4.8&nbsp;million by Sustainable Development Technology Canada (SDTC)&nbsp;for a project to
further develop fuel cell power module technology for the transit bus market. Design improvements
will be implemented on test buses to be operated in Metro Vancouver, beginning in the fourth
quarter of 2010.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">&#147;The Government of Canada is committed to supporting alternate sources of energy, including
hydrogen,&#148; said the Honourable Stockwell Day, President of the Treasury Board and Minister for the
Asia-Pacific Gateway. &#147;By supporting innovative hydrogen and fuel cell research projects, such as
Ballard&#146;s power module, we are putting more zero-emissions vehicles on the road while creating high
quality jobs and improving the health of Canadians.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">&#147;Heavy duty diesel vehicles account for almost half of Canada&#146;s road transportation greenhouse gas
emissions,&#148; said Vicky Sharpe, President and CEO of SDTC. &#147;The technology developed by Ballard will
increase the cost-effectiveness and performance of fuel cell hybrid buses, making them more
accessible to public transit authorities and helping Canada to reduce its greenhouse gas
emissions.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">Michael Goldstein, Ballard&#146;s Chief Commercial Officer added &#147;This level of support from the
Canadian federal government and SDTC will have a measurable impact on the evolution of clean energy
fuel cell products for commercial mass transit applications here in Canada and in locations around
the globe.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">In the development project funded by SDTC, Ballard and it&#146;s system integration partner, ISE
Corporation, will design, assemble and test key sub-components for Ballard&#146;s FCvelocity&#153;-HD6 power
module and the hybrid electric drive system. Refinement of this critical new technology will
facilitate the commercial introduction of fuel cell hybrid buses by reducing cost, improving
durability and robustness of select sub-systems, and improving overall bus performance.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">Ballard&#146;s power module is a &#145;plug-and-play&#146; fuel cell-based product that enables system integrators
to build clean energy buses more easily and at lower cost. The fuel cell hybrid platform eliminates
all emissions from bus tailpipes, while meeting the demands of range and duty cycle for virtually
any bus route.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt"><B>About Ballard Power Systems</B>
<BR>
Ballard Power Systems (TSX: BLD; NASDAQ: BLDP) provides clean energy fuel cell products enabling
optimized power systems for a range of applications. To learn more about Ballard, please visit
www.ballard.com.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt"><B>About SDTC</B>
<BR>
Sustainable Development Technology Canada (SDTC)&nbsp;is an arm&#146;s-length foundation created by the
Government of Canada which has received $1.05&nbsp;billion as part of the Government&#146;s commitment to
create a healthy environment and a high quality of life for all Canadians.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">SDTC operates two funds aimed at the development and demonstration of innovative technological
solutions. The $550&nbsp;million SD Tech Fund&#153; supports projects that address climate change, air
quality, clean water, and clean soil. The $500&nbsp;million NextGen Biofuels Fund&#153; supports the
establishment of first-of-kind large demonstration-scale facilities for the production of
next-generation renewable fuels.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">SDTC operates as a not-for-profit corporation and has been working with the public and private
sector including industry, academia, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), the financial community
and all levels of government to achieve this mandate.
