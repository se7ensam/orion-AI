<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">News Release


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Ballard Passes Key Milestone for Hydrogen Fuel Cell Deployments in India


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For Immediate Release &#150; July&nbsp;2, 2009


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">VANCOUVER, CANADA&#150; Ballard Power Systems (TSX: BLD; NASDAQ: BLDP) announced today that together
with its partner, IdaTech LLC, it has passed a product acceptance milestone and confirmed the order
of 310 ElectraGen<sup>TM</sup> H2 hydrogen-based units. These units will be used to provide backup
power for wireless telecommunications network equipment to ACME Tele Power Ltd customers in the
Indian market. The units were designed and built around Ballard&#146;s FCgen<sup>TM</sup>&#150;1020 ACS fuel
cell stack.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">John Sheridan, Ballard&#146;s President and Chief Executive Officer said, &#147;Having our
FCgen<sup>TM</sup>-1020 ACS fuel cell product pass product acceptance as part of IdaTech&#146;s
ElectraGen<sup>TM</sup> H2 DC generator is a key proof point in our joint partnership with ACME.
This formal acceptance triggers the order of 310 hydrogen units representing around 2MW of total
power. Under the terms of the contract announced in October&nbsp;2008, an order of natural gas units
representing 10,000 units or over 70MW of total power is to follow later in 2009, subject to
product acceptance tests on our new natural gas product.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">In support of the future natural gas deployment, Ballard is developing a new low-cost,
reformate-capable FCgen<sup>TM</sup>-1300 fuel cell product. This technology overcomes distribution
challenges presented with hydrogen, particularly in remote areas in India. In addition, the
technology takes advantage of government subsidies for natural gas, accessing significant natural
gas reserves and leveraging a growing natural gas distribution network throughout the country.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">IdaTech&#146;s ElectraGen<sup>TM</sup> NG DC power generator, fueled by natural gas, will be built
incorporating Ballard&#146;s new FCgen<sup>TM</sup>-1300 fuel cell. Ballard plans to begin shipping its
FCgen<sup>TM</sup>-1300 fuel cell product in the last quarter of 2009.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;The news that the ElectraGen<sup>TM</sup> H2 has been accepted by ACME and its customers is a very
exciting development for IdaTech and, we believe for the industry as a whole.&nbsp; This order further
validates the use of fuel cells as a viable and economic alternative to diesel generators,&#148; said
Hal Koyama, CEO of Idatech.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;The performance of the ElectraGen<sup>TM</sup> H2 unit, incorporating Ballard&#146;s
FCgen<sup>TM</sup>-1020 ACS fuel cell, passed our acceptance criteria and expectations,&#148; stated
Sanjay Dhawan, CEO, ACME Tele Power Ltd. &nbsp;Atul Sabharwal, Chief Operating Officer, ACME Group,
further commented, &#147;We are excited to be working with both Ballard and IdaTech. &nbsp;The strong
performance of the hydrogen fuel cells is an important operational milestone and demonstrates that
we are on track for the broader deployment of the&nbsp;natural gas fuel cell product to begin later in
the year.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Extended duration backup power for wireless cell phone towers is a necessity in India, where due to
an unstable electrical grid, daily extended power outages are common. Fuel cells provide extended
run times, longer lifetimes and lower overall maintenance costs over the incumbent diesel generator
and lead acid battery solutions. In addition, fuel cell backup power systems reduce noise and air
emissions along with avoiding disposal issues associated with lead acid batteries.
