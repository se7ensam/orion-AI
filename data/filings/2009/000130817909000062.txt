<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:20pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt"><B><U>EXECUTIVE COMMENTARY</U></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial">Ben Verwaayen, CEO, commented: </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#147;This quarter was about putting together the new Alcatel-Lucent. I am pleased with the customer response to our new direction and strategy.<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>Their confidence in our capabilities is strong,<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>as illustrated by our recent wins in 3G and LTE as well as the encouraging increase in our order intake in both North America and Asia Pacific&#148;. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#147;As we discussed before, 2009 will be a year of transition. We are reshaping the company and aggressively pursuing our product portfolio rationalization, co-sourcing, working capital management and SG&amp;A reduction programs&#148;. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#147;While expected, given seasonality and tough market conditions, we are not pleased with the operating loss incurred in the first quarter. Our guidance for the year remains unchanged and we are taking appropriate actions&#148;. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B><U><BR>
<BR></U></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:18pt; font-family:Arial" align=right>1</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:12pt"><BR></P>
<HR style="padding-top:7.2pt; padding-bottom:7.2pt" noshade size=1.333>
<P style="page-break-before:always; margin:0pt; line-height:14pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt"><B><U>KEY HIGHLIGHTS</U></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#149;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>First quarter revenue decreased 6.9% year-over-year and 27.4% sequentially to Euro 3.598 billion</B>. &nbsp;At constant currency exchange rates, revenue fell by 11.2% year-over-year and 28.3% sequentially, with a mixed performance among each of the four segments. On the one hand, Carrier and Enterprise revenues declined at double-digit rates year-over-year at constant currency, reflecting capital constraints in fixed and mobile access, switching and enterprise voice telephony, only partly offset by the growth in IP, W-CDMA and submarine optics. On the other hand, Applications software revenues grew at a mid-single digit rate at constant currency while Services revenues grew in the high teens.<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>From a geographic standpoint, the decline is largely attributable to North America where revenues decreased 28% year-over-year at constant currency and to a lesser extent Asia 
Pacific (-8%), while Europe (-1.5%) and the rest of the world (+1%) showed good resilience.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#149;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Adjusted operating income of Euro (254) million or (7.1%) of revenue. </B>Adjusted gross margin came in at 31.5% of revenue for the quarter, down from 36.2% in the year ago quarter and 33.3% in the fourth quarter 2008. Both the year-over-year and sequential declines in gross margin were largely driven by volumes and the product/geographic mix. The year-over-year decline was also impacted by the recovery of the US dollar as well as the non recurrence of the hedging gain booked in the year ago quarter (90bp impact on the gross margin). Operating expenses were up slightly year-over-year and sequentially, reflecting the strong reduction in R&amp;D capitalization, the non recurrence of the one time items (sale of patents) booked in both the first quarter 2008 and in the fourth quarter 2008 and finally (on a year-over-year basis only), the recovery of the US dollar. Excluding the impact of capitali
zation and one time items, gross R&amp;D expenses were flat year over year and up 3.3% sequentially. SG&amp;A expenses fell 3.7% year-over-year and 4.7% sequentially. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#149;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Net debt of Euro (841) million, versus Euro (389) million as of December 31, 2008. </B>The sequential increase in net debt of Euro (452) million primarily reflects the adjusted operating loss reported in the quarter, interest expenses of Euro (75)<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>million, restructuring cash outlays of Euro (178) million and contribution to Pensions and OPEB of Euro (50) million. Effective January 1<SUP style="font-size:7pt">st</SUP> 2009, operating cash flow (now defined as cash generated from operations <U>after</U> changes in working capital <U>but before</U> interest/tax paid, restructuring cash outlay and pension &amp; OPEB outlay) has been introduced as a key metric for management compensation. For the quarter, the operating cash flow was Euro (43) million, compared to Euro 131 million in the first quarter 2008 and Euro 663 million in the fourth quarter 2008. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#149;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Material debt repayment carried out in the first quarter</B>. Alcatel-Lucent paid-off the 4.375% bond due February 2009 for a nominal value of Euro 777 million. The company repurchased at a substantial discount USD 99 million (nominal value) of the Lucent 7.75% USD convertible bond due March 2017. With<B> </B>cash and marketable securities of Euro 3.34 billion as of March 31<SUP style="font-size:7pt">st</SUP> 2009, the imminent sale of its stake in Thales and a Euro 1.4 billion credit line, Alcatel-Lucent remains adequately funded.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#149;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Funded status of Pensions and OPEB of Euro (545) million at end March, </B>compared to Euro (429) million as of December 31, 2008. &nbsp;This slight sequential widening of the deficit mainly reflects a decline in plan assets, almost offset by the reduction in the net present value of benefit obligations due to an increase in the discount rate for US pensions. The final assessment of private equity and real estate assets as of December 31, 2008 confirmed that all large US plans remained overfunded from an ERISA perspective at the end of last year. Alcatel-Lucent thus reiterates its earlier statement that it does not expect any funding contribution through 2010. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B><U><BR>
<BR></U></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:18pt; font-family:Arial" align=right>2</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:12pt"><BR></P>
<HR style="padding-top:7.2pt; padding-bottom:7.2pt" noshade size=1.333>
<P style="page-break-before:always; margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B><U><BR></U></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:20pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt"><B><U>OUTLOOK AND PROGRESS ON STRATEGIC PLAN</U></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Alcatel-Lucent reiterates its guidance for 2009. </B>The company continues to expect the global telecommunications equipment and related services market to be down between 8% and 12% at constant currency in 2009. The company still anticipates an adjusted operating profit around break-even in 2009. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Progress
  on cost reduction plan</B>: As far as simplifying the organization structure
  is concerned, 290 management positions have been eliminated out of the 1,000
  planned. The number of contractors has been reduced by about 770 out of the
  5,000 plan. In all, the company still projects that, by the fourth quarter 2009,
  on an annual run rate basis, it should achieve total cost and expense savings
  of Euro 750 million at constant exchange rate.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B><BR></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Alcatel-Lucent is actively sharpening its portfolio around its &quot;high leverage network&quot;&nbsp;strategy, </B>which aims at ensuring continuous and cost-effective scaling of bandwidth from the access to the transport layer, while instrumenting<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>the network with built-in service and application awareness as well as traffic&nbsp;optimization capabilities. Combined with its expertise in application enablers, enterprise solutions and integration services, Alcatel-Lucent believes that the high leverage network will enable its carrier customers to deliver and manage&nbsp;both their own as well as third-party advanced applications, compelling content and personalized services to residential, business and mobile users. For example, the company is currently: </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:36pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#149;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:36pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>boosting its investment in next generation wireless access (LTE, W-CDMA, converged RAN), fixed access (VDSL, GPON), IP (Service routers, Carrier Ethernet, Evolved packet core, IMS) and optics (WDM, Microwave packet radio, Packet optical transport); in 2009, these segments are expected to account for slightly less than 60% of our R&amp;D investments versus slightly more than 40% in 2008;</P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:36pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#149;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:36pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>Rationalizing its spend to optimize margins and cash returns in mature platforms (ATM-based ADSL, TDM switching, CDMA 1x, GSM, ATM, legacy optics); &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:36pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#149;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:36pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>Reducing exposure to the segments where it lacks scale or where the market opportunity is smaller than anticipated (Mobile<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>WiMAX, CPE, non IMS-based fixed NGN, some legacy applications).</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:3pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Creating a more agile company</B>. Alcatel-Lucent is engaged in active discussions with potential co-sourcing partners. The aim is to develop a joint go-to-market approach to leverage the IT/telecommunications convergence and help Alcatel-Lucent optimize its efficiency in areas such as IS/IT, finance, HR and R&amp;D. </P>

<P style="page-break-before:always; margin:0pt; line-height:14pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt"><B><U>BUSINESS COMMENTARY </U></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B><BR></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B>CARRIER SEGMENT</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:4.95pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>For
  the first quarter 2009, revenues for the Carrier segment were Euro 2.219<B>
  </B>billion, a decrease of 14.0% compared to Euro 2.581 billion in the year-ago
  quarter and a decrease of 29.1% compared to Euro 3.129 billion in the fourth
  quarter 2008. At constant currency exchange rates, Carrier revenues decreased
  18.3% year-over-year and 30.7%<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>sequentially. The
  segment posted an adjusted<SUP style="font-size:7pt">2</SUP> operating<SUP style="font-size:7pt">1</SUP> loss of Euro (154)
  million or an operating margin of (6.9) % compared to a loss of Euro (2)<FONT COLOR=#FF0000>
  </FONT>million or a margin of (0.1)<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>% in the year
  ago period. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><B>Key highlights: </B></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-12pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Good growth in IP. </B>Revenues for the IP division were Euro 287 million, an increase of 4.7% from the year ago quarter. Alcatel-Lucent enjoyed another quarter of solid growth in IP/MPLS service routers, with particular strength in EMEA, partly offset by the continuing decline in ATM revenues. Alcatel-Lucent continues to invest in its IP portfolio to expand its addressable market: on April 1<SUP style="font-size:7pt">st</SUP>, leveraging its unique strengths in IP and Wireless, the company announced the introduction of a new packet core gateway based on the 7750 Service Router, a product which is expected to be used as one of the key elements of the evolved packet Core in the Verizon LTE roll-out. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-12pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Resilience in Optics: </B>Revenues for the Optics division were Euro 657 million,<B> </B>a 2.1% decline from the year ago quarter, with contrasted market dynamics. On the one hand, revenues from long distance Dense-Wave Division Multiplexing (D-WDM), optical cross connects and wireless transmission declined. On the other, metro aggregation grew at a double-digit rate and submarine optics grew strongly. &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-12pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Wireless impacted by the decline in 2G:</B> Revenues for the Wireless Networks division were Euro 911 million, a decline of 18.0%<B> </B>from the year ago quarter. CDMA revenues declined materially which was driven largely by lower sales in North America, only partially offset by the roll-out of EV-DO in China. GSM also declined significantly, as slower economic growth and in some cases currency devaluations impacted this activity in Asia-Pacific, the Middle East and Africa. W-CDMA revenue increased very strongly, driven by North America and initial roll-outs in China. Finally, the company&#146;s LTE solution is getting increased traction in the market place: in addition to its selection as one of the two vendors for Verizon, Alcatel-Lucent was shortlisted in several LTE trials this quarter.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-12pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Slowdown in access and switching</B>: Revenues for the Wireline networks division were Euro 394 million, a decline of 28.4% from the year ago quarter. This decline was largely driven by legacy ADSL access and core switching, partially offset by the growth in VDSL and home networking (DSL CPE and GPON ONTs). Alcatel-Lucent&#146;s IMS solution continued to gain traction and was selected by 4 new customers this quarter, including FT/Orange as part of its planned replacement of H323 technology by a SIP based platform for its VoIP services. &nbsp;</P>

<P style="page-break-before:always; margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B>APPLICATIONS SOFTWARE</B> <B>SEGMENT</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:4.95pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:4.95pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>For the first quarter 2009, revenues for the Applications software segment were Euro 255<B> </B>million, an increase of 13.3% compared to Euro 225 million in the year-ago quarter and a decrease of 22.5% compared to Euro 329 million in the fourth quarter 2008. At constant currency exchange rates, Applications software revenues increased 4.5% year-over-year and decreased 24.5% sequentially. The segment posted an adjusted<SUP style="font-size:7pt">2</SUP> operating<SUP style="font-size:7pt">1</SUP> loss of Euro (26) million or an operating margin of (10.2) % compared to an adjusted<SUP style="font-size:7pt">2</SUP> operating<SUP style="font-size:7pt">1</SUP> loss of Euro (24) million or a margin of (10.7) % a year ago. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><B>Key highlights</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>Carrier applications revenue grew at a double-digit rate this quarter, which was largely driven by rich communications solutions (IMS applications and messaging) in North America, the successful integration of the Motive portfolio and to a lesser extent payment and subscriber data management in Asia-Pacific, fuelled by wireless subscriber growth and the roll of 3G in China. Alcatel-Lucent secured a relevant number of design wins this quarter, mostly around rich communications, highlighting a strong response from operators, especially in North America, to the initial roll-out of its applications enablement strategy. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>Genesys, the contact centre software activity, saw a decline in revenues this quarter due to the combination of strong results in the year-ago quarter, when a major contract was landed with a large European operator, and the slow-down in corporate investment. The profitability of the segment was roughly unchanged this quarter, as the cost reduction actions were offset by the reduced contribution from Genesys to revenues.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B><BR></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B>ENTERPRISE SEGMENT</B></P>

<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:4.95pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>For the first quarter 2009, revenues for the Enterprise segment were Euro 245 million, a decrease of 17.5% compared to Euro 297 million in the year-ago quarter and a decrease of 23.0% compared to Euro 318 million in the fourth quarter 2008. At constant currency exchange rates, Enterprise revenues decreased 19.5% year-over-year and 23.1% sequentially. The segment posted an Adjusted<SUP style="font-size:7pt">2</SUP> operating<SUP style="font-size:7pt">1</SUP> loss of Euro (36) million, or (14.7) % of revenues compared to a profit of Euro 11 million or 3.7 % in the year-ago quarter. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><B>Key highlights: </B></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Revenues from Enterprise solutions declined in the low teens this quarter, as constraints in corporate investment impacted the voice telephony market, primarily in the SMB space, while the large enterprise market proved more resilient. Revenues were approximately stable in data networking and grew in security solutions and unified communications. </P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Revenues from Industrial components were the most impacted by the global market conditions and declined at a material rate this quarter.</P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>From a geographic standpoint, Europe and Asia Pacific were the weakest, while North America saw a limited decline and Central and Latin America enjoyed slight growth.</P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>The operating loss incurred by the segment is largely due to volumes and, to a lesser extent, mix.</P>

<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><B><BR></B></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Managed &amp; Outsourcing solutions grew very strongly this quarter, driven by the many large contracts signed throughout the year 2008 and which had not come into effect in the first quarter of last year.
