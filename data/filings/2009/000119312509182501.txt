<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"> <P ALIGN="left"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Joint venture to initially focus on variable annuities </FONT></P></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman"
SIZE="2">The life insurance joint venture between AEGON and Sony Life has obtained final approval from the Japanese regulatory authorities to launch business operations later this year. The 50-50 joint venture &#150; known as AEGON Sony Life
Insurance Co., Ltd. (ASLIC) &#150; will initially focus on the sale of variable annuity products which will be distributed through Sony Life&#146;s &#145;Lifeplanner&#146; sales employees, as well as banks and other financial institutions. ASLIC is
expected to begin operations in early December. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;We&#146;re pleased that the regulatory authorities in Japan have granted a license to our joint
venture,&#148; said AEGON CEO Alex Wynaendts. &#147;We believe that the market for variable annuities in Japan offers strong prospects for growth over the long-term. By combining our product knowledge and risk management expertise with Sony
Life&#146;s highly respected brand name and distribution capabilities, we are in a strong position to maximize the opportunities of the Japanese market&#148;. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman"
SIZE="2">&#147;We established ASLIC as a life insurance company which focuses and specializes in annuity products,&#148; said Sony Life&#146;s President Taro Okuda. &#147;Through the operations of ASLIC, we shall fully enter into the bancassurance
business in Japan. At the same time, annuity products produced by ASLIC will make Sony Life&#146;s &#145;Lifeplanner&#146; sales employees offer all the more solid financial services for the customers&#148;. </FONT></P> <P
STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Sony Life regards the partnership, established in 2007, as an opportunity to further strengthen its &#145;Lifeplanner&#146; distribution network, particularly by
broadening the range of financial products available to customers in Japan. For AEGON, the joint venture with Sony Life marks another step in expanding its operations in Asia. In recent years, the company has built up operations in both China and
India, where economic growth and rising levels of personal wealth have led to increased demand for life insurance, pensions and other long-term investment products. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Japan is one of the largest pension markets in the world. Despite the financial crisis, demand for variable annuities in Japan is expected to grow in the years ahead, particularly as the country&#146;s population ages
and more savers look to supplement existing state pensions. Japan already has one of the oldest populations in the world, with more than 40% of the population currently over the age of fifty. In addition, a significant proportion of individual
savings is held either in cash or on deposit. Japan has the largest pool of personal financial assets in Asia, which is projected to reach nearly USD 19 trillion in 2017. </FONT></P>
