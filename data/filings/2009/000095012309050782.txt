<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt"><B>PGG Wrightson and Agria announce formation of strategic partnership and intention for Agria to be a
new cornerstone shareholder of PGG Wrightson</B>
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Auckland, New Zealand; and Beijing, China &#151; PGG Wrightson (NZX ticker PGW) and Agria Corporation
(NYSE: GRO) (&#147;Agria&#148;) today announced their signing of an agreement that provides for Agria to
make an investment in PGG Wrightson (the &#147;Subscription Agreement&#148;) and for the formation of a
strategic partnership between PGG Wrightson and Agria (the &#147;Co-operation Agreement&#148;).
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">PGG Wrightson is New Zealand&#146;s largest rural services business, offering a wide range of products,
services and solutions to farmers, growers and processors in New Zealand and internationally.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Agria (NYSE: GRO) is a China-based agricultural solutions provider listed on the New York Stock
Exchange and engaged in research and development, production and sale of upstream agricultural
products.
</DIV>

</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">PGG Wrightson and Agria have entered into a strategic partnership and have jointly undertaken to
work to create value for both companies through the advancement of agricultural technology and the
development of new markets. The scope of co-operation includes:

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>Comments</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The chairman of PGG Wrightson, Mr Keith Smith, said the arrangements being progressed with Agria
had the potential to create significant long-term value for both companies.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&#147;The Co-operation Agreement identifies a range of opportunities for PGG Wrightson and Agria to work
together to create value. It will provide a framework in which intellectual property, management
know-how and financial resources can be deployed jointly and for mutual benefit, according to
specific opportunities and requirements.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&#147;This programme will be very consistent with the existing business platform of PGG Wrightson. Our
primary focus will remain on our customers in New Zealand, Australia and South America &#151; and on
improving performance in our existing businesses and markets while exploring new opportunities
under the Co-operation Agreement. Agria is investing on the same basis.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&#147;PGG Wrightson has for some time been looking to establish a strategy and business platform for
entry into China. This agreement and the relationship with Agria will enable that aspiration to be
met. PGG Wrightson, along with others in agribusiness, has been evaluating for some time the scale
and types of opportunities available through China&#146;s emergence as a global agricultural force.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&#147;We have now identified a partner whose business profile and intentions are truly compatible with
our own. From PGG Wrightson&#146;s perspective, the partnership embodied in the Co-operation Agreement
will enable entry into specific markets and facilitate growth alongside a partner with a unique
market footprint and strategic relationships.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&#147;We also look forward to welcoming Agria as a new cornerstone shareholder. This is entirely
consistent with the programme to be undertaken through the Co-operation Agreement, and will clearly
assist PGG Wrightson in meeting its financial objectives.&#148;
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Mr.&nbsp;Alan Lai, Agria Corporation&#146;s Chairman, commented: &#147;We are excited to be working with PGG
Wrightson. The strategic co-operation agreement gives Agria the opportunity to work alongside a
high-quality management team with world-leading experience in the agriculture sector. The areas of
co-operation have been carefully chosen as those where combining the technical, commercial and
operational experience of the two businesses gives greatest opportunity for commercial success in
China, New Zealand and internationally.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&#147;China never has a lack of growth opportunities and market potential but success requires hands on
operational expertise that this partnership brings.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&#147;The quality of international operational expertise to which Agria will gain access is not
available in agricultural services in China, and will serve to strengthen Agria&#146;s capabilities in
the implementation of its strategic direction.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&#147;China is a world leader in many areas of agriculture, including cultivation of rice, cotton and to
a certain extent corn, and Agria has secured access to world class research through relationships
with leading research institutes including China National Academy of Agricultural Sciences. I
believe by partnering with PGG Wrightson we can best accelerate the commercialisation of these
opportunities.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&#147;Co-operation between China and New Zealand is highly logical given the importance of the
agriculture sector to both countries. This view is supported by the free-trade agreement that China
has entered into with New Zealand, which is China&#146;s first and currently only free trade agreement
with a developed country.&#148;
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt"><B>Information on PGG Wrightson and Agria</B>
</DIV>

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><!-- Folio -->&nbsp;<!-- /Folio -->

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">PGG Wrightson (<u>www.pggwrightson.co.nz</u>) is a publicly traded company, listed on the New Zealand
Stock Exchange under the ticker &#147;PGW&#148;, with annual turnover of NZ$1.3&nbsp;billion dollars. The company
is New Zealand&#146;s largest rural services business, offering a wide range of products, services and
solutions to farmers, growers and processors in New Zealand, and to processors and end-users
internationally. PGG Wrightson holds market leading positions in most of its businesses, which
include seeds, livestock, animal nutrition, farm supplies, finance, real estate, irrigation and
training: and through joint ventures in wool and insurance. PGG Wrightson draws on the knowledge
and expertise of 2,700 staff &#151; and the services of diverse business units, alliances and
partnerships &#151; to meet the needs of more than 90,000 clients across New Zealand. The company has
growing operations in Australia and South America. It was formed in October&nbsp;2005 through the
merger of Pyne Gould Guinness and Wrightson. PGG and Wrightson dated from 1851 and 1841
respectively.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">New York Stock Exchange listed Agria Corporation, ticker &#147;GRO&#148;, is an innovative China-based
agri-solutions provider focusing on research and development, production and distribution of three
different types of upstream agricultural products. Its diversified portfolio of products comprises
corn seeds, sheep breeding and seedlings, including proprietary products. Agria processes and
packages corn seed products and sells them to local and regional distributors. It produces and
sells sheep breeding products (frozen semen, embryos, breeder sheep and <I>Primalights III </I>hybrid
sheep) primarily to government-operated breed improvement and reproductive stations, breeding
companies and other sheep reproductive stations and farms. The Company also produces and sells
blackberry, raspberry and date seedlings to end users such as municipal agencies and seedling
companies; and white bark pine seedlings for urban greenery. Agria has a strong research and
development base and extensive relationships throughout China&#146;s agricultural sector, including
preferential access to one of the largest seedbanks in the world through a joint venture with the
China National Academy of Agricultural Sciences (CNAAS). CNAAS is one of the most important
research groups in China, owning 39 research institutes covering all major areas in the
agricultural sector. CNAAS has chosen Agria to commercialise its research and technology through a
majority equity investment in Zhongnon (China Agricultural Seed Company) and a strategic
co-operation agreement between CNAAS and Agria. Agria&#146;s website is www.agriacorp.com.

<DIV align="Center" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt"><B>Strategic Cooperation Agreement &#151; Summary</B>

</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>Rationale for co-operation agreement</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">PGG Wrightson Limited (PGW)&nbsp;and Agria Corporation (Agria) have agreed to establish a long-term
relationship through a strategic Co-operation Agreement.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The agreement will promote co-investment by Agria and PGW, allowing Agria to work alongside a
proven management team with world leading experience in agriculture.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">PGW and Agria have agreed to enter into strategic cooperation in specific areas, and also expect
that other opportunities may arise from time to time. They have committed to develop the principles
of the Co-operation Agreement into actionable business plans and delivery of tangible financial
benefits.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">As an overriding principle of co-operation the parties agree that this should be through joint
ventures in and out of China. These will be held in proportion to the ratio of capital contributed
but in no case would one of the parties hold less than 30&nbsp;percent unless by mutual consent. Both
parties agree that the joint ventures may necessarily involve additional parties.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The Parties will further evaluate the types of products/services, total investment, project
locations, distribution channels, ownership of intellectual property, branding and marketing, and
other factors for the project.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>Areas for co-operation</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt"><B>1. Seeds</B>

</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Joint development and commercialisation of cultivars that Agria, PGW, and their development
partners have access to internationally. This would see the licence of intellectual property held
by PGW licensed in China and internationally, and give PGW access to Chinese intellectual property.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">PGW has development partners in the form of joint ventures and collaborations in New Zealand,
Australia, Argentina, Brazil, France, the United Kingdom, the United States and Uruguay.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Agria has an extensive relationship with the China National Academy of Agricultural Science
(&#147;CNAAS&#148;) and through its strategic shareholding in Zhongnon (China Agricultural Seed Company), a
company jointly owned by Agria and CNAAS to commercialise CNAAS intellectual property.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">PGW and Agria have agreed to co-operate in commercialisation of cultivars where they have existing
distribution channels or access to new markets and territories.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Initially, access to the Chinese market may be provided through Agria&#146;s existing operations; and
later through joint ventures.
</DIV>

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><!-- Folio -->&nbsp;<!-- /Folio -->
</DIV>

<DIV align="right" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt">&nbsp;6
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The parties have agreed to expand CNAAS advanced technologies for development of seed cultivars in
China and other territories.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>2. Livestock</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">PGW and Agria have agreed that New Zealand has exceptional livestock experience which can be
utilised in China. The form of co-operation envisaged is for Agria to source livestock needs in
China and for PGW to fill those needs through live export, from New Zealand, Uruguay, and selected
markets.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Additionally, the parties will co-operate to promote reform in livestock trading in China. Using
New Zealand&#146;s and PGW&#146;s technical experience with livestock auction systems, the parties will work
to initiate reform in these markets, specifically the establishment of an auction system in China.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>3. Agricultural Services and Science Development</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Agria and PGW will work to develop research farms in China that will seek to exhibit and test
specific farming practices, systems and technologies, focussing on grass type, stock varieties,
irrigation and animal health practices.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">With a view to enhancing productivity in countries and areas of joint focus, the parties will
explore opportunities to foster collaboration between New Zealand agriculture based tertiary
institutions (and other institutions with which PGW has relationships around the world) and Chinese
agricultural and science universities. Such collaboration will be in respect of, but not limited
to, the development of commercially focussed research and learning in:
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>4. Finance</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Agria will work with PGG Wrightson Finance to identify additional funding options for growth
through third party sources. The benefit for the funding provider will be access to lending
expertise in the rural finance sector.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>5. Rural Services &#151; China</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">PGW and Agria have agreed that, as the Chinese agricultural market does not have a mature provider
of rural services, they will carry out investigations on development of a rural services business
in China using a joint venture model.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The business will necessarily be different from the New Zealand based business, but will draw
heavily from PGW management experience and expertise, with capital being largely provided by Agria
and its Chinese connections.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The initial focus of such a joint venture will be rural merchandise, irrigation and pumping
services, warehousing, marketing and export, and specialist advice.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt"><B>6. Dairy Farm conversion and development</B>
</DIV>

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><!-- Folio -->&nbsp;<!-- /Folio -->
</DIV>

<P><HR noshade><P>
<H5 align="left" style="page-break-before:always">&nbsp;</H5><P>

<DIV style="font-family: Helvetica,Arial,sans-serif">

<DIV align="right" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt">&nbsp;7
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Agria and PGW believe there is great merit to certain aspects of the business model encapsulated in
the NZ Farming Systems Uruguay model, which is based upon a large scale dairy conversion managed by
PGW. The Parties will seek to replicate such a model in other regions and countries where the
business case can be proven, including China, with the intention that Agria will have a first call
option for the purchase of produce provided that it is destined for China.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The basis for joint ventures in this area will be to use PGW management and Chinese capital. Agria
will endeavour to acquire the necessary land within the PRC to apply this business model.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>7. Co-operation in PRC-financed cross-border agricultural investment</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">PGW and Agria agree to collaborate in future investment in agricultural investment worldwide,
including further investment in New Zealand and South America, through financing obtained within
the PRC. Agria agrees to endeavour to solicit and obtain financing and loans from domestic PRC
financial institutions for possible reinvestment abroad.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">In addition PGW and Agria agree on a best endeavours basis to identify methods of securing China
based investment capital to PGW businesses (and/or PGW and Agria joint ventures) where this may
provide a competitive advantage to PGW and Agria, or enable growth of a specific business unit.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>Implementation</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The Parties will establish a steering committee comprised of six (6)&nbsp;members from the senior
management of each. The Steering Committee will have the overall responsibility of determining and
overseeing the parties&#146; co-operative projects.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>Term</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The Co-operation Agreement is for a period of ten (10)&nbsp;years. It can be terminated early or
extended by mutual agreement, or by one party in circumstances specified in the Agreement.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">16 October&nbsp;2009
