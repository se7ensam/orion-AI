<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">News Release


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Ballard Announces Distribution Agreement for Telecom Backup Power in India


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For Immediate Release &#150; November&nbsp;16, 2009


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">VANCOUVER, CANADA&#150; Ballard Power Systems (TSX: BLD; NASDAQ: BLDP) today announced that its partners
IdaTech, LLC (&#147;IdaTech&#148;) and ACME Group (&#147;ACME&#148;) have terminated the existing supply agreement
between the three parties and replaced it with a new, more flexible distribution agreement between
IdaTech and ACME for fuel cell solutions for wireless telecom backup power in India. Under the
terms of this new agreement, Acme can distribute a range of IdaTech fuel cell products, focused on
the natural gas system currently under development at IdaTech. The IdaTech natural gas fuel cell
product development, incorporating Ballard&#146;s FCgen<sup>TM </sup>1300 stack, continues with good
progress, as reported by IdaTech today. Pricing and volume in the agreement are flexible and based
on local market conditions. Shipment of the ElectraGen<sup>TM</sup> hydrogen systems, incorporating
Ballard&#146;s FCgen<sup>TM</sup> 1020 fuel cell stacks, under the original Supply Agreement is
unaffected by this change.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Atul Sabharwal, Chief Operating Officer, ACME Group said, &#147;ACME is pleased to have a new
distribution agreement with IdaTech that will enable the deployment of fuel cell solutions in
wireless telecom backup power in India. We feel as strongly as ever that a substantial market
opportunity exists for fuel cells in India.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Hal Koyama, IdaTech&#146;s Chief Executive Officer said, &#147;We are looking forward to supplying ACME with
our ElectraGen<sup>TM </sup>natural gas and hydrogen systems, both of which incorporate Ballard&#146;s
fuel cell stacks. There are tremendous opportunities for both of these products in
telecommunications backup power in India and beyond.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">John Sheridan, Ballard&#146;s President and Chief Executive Officer said, &#147;We continue to see clear
evidence of the large market opportunity in India and are pleased to be moving forward with this
ACME-IdaTech-Ballard partnership.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Background


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">As previously disclosed, the original agreement was subject to meeting product acceptance testing
(PAT)&nbsp;timelines. On July&nbsp;2, 2009, Ballard announced that, together with its partner IdaTech, it had
passed a PAT milestone and confirmed the order of 310 ElectraGen<sup>TM</sup> H2 hydrogen-based
systems, incorporating 620 Ballard FCgen<sup>TM</sup>&#150;1020 ACS fuel cell stacks, for ACME customers
in the Indian market. These direct hydrogen units are not impacted by the new distribution
agreement for natural gas systems. On September&nbsp;23, 2009, IdaTech announced it would likely miss
the PAT for the natural gas system, scheduled for October&nbsp;16, 2009.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">About Ballard Power Systems


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Ballard Power Systems (TSX: BLD; NASDAQ: BLDP) is recognized as a world leader in the design,
development, manufacture and sale of clean energy fuel cell products. Ballard&#146;s mission is to
accelerate fuel cell product adoption. To learn more about what Ballard is doing with Power to
Change the World&#174;, visit www.ballard.com.
