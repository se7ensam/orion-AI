<P style="page-break-before:always; margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B><U>EXECUTIVE COMMENTARY</U></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial">Ben Verwaayen, CEO, commented: </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#147;In the fourth quarter, we did what we said we were going to do. I am encouraged by our operating performance, measured by our ability to achieve our top-line, operating margin and cash flow targets. The asset impairment charge that severely impacted our bottom line was made necessary by the drastic deterioration of the global economic outlook during the fourth quarter as well our decision to shift to a more focused portfolio. With an improving balance sheet, adequate funding, a new strategy in place and a clear roadmap to profitability, we are committed to executing on our plans to deliver better solutions and services to our customers and better returns to our shareholders&#148;.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B><U>KEY HIGHLIGHTS</U></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>Alcatel-Lucent delivered solid operational performance in the fourth quarter, enabling it to meet its guidance on all three of the financial metrics it had indicated for 2008.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#149;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>The company met its revenue guidance</B>. The reported revenue decline of 4.5% in 2008 - within the indicated range of low to mid single-digit &#150; is largely attributable to the shift in currencies. At constant currency exchange rates, revenue fell by 1.1% in 2008, in an economic environment that grew considerably more challenging in the latter part of the year. At constant currency exchange rates, fourth quarter revenue declined 7.5% year-over-year and increased by 16.9% sequentially. As anticipated, carrier revenues were impacted by some capital expenditure constraints in the fourth quarter, notably in fixed and mobile access. This was partially offset, however, by the strong performance of other carrier segments such as IP routing, submarine and Next Generation Networks, the resilience of the Enterprise business and solid growth in Services. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#149;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>The operating profitability target was achieved. </B>Adjusted gross margin came in at 34.1% for the full year, at the lower end of the mid-thirties range which had been indicated, after a slight sequential improvement in the fourth quarter. Coupled with the reduction in operating expenses, this led to an adjusted operating margin of 2.7% for 2008, within the low to mid single-digit guidance range which had been indicated at the start of the year. The fourth quarter adjusted operating profit reached 6.0% including the net positive impact of 0.2<B> </B>percentage point from one-time items. &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-14pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>&#149;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:18pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>The cash flow target was achieved</B>: At year end 2008, net debt was brought down to Euro (389) million, below the end-June level of Euro (415) million as indicated, thanks to an operating cash flow<SUP style=font-size:7pt>3</SUP> of Euro 658 million in the fourth quarter, the highest since the close of the merger. The divestiture of the stake in Thales for Euro 1.6 billion is proceeding on plan. With cash and marketable securities of Euro 4.6 billion and the availability of the Euro 1.4 billion credit line, Alcatel-Lucent remains adequately funded.</P>

<P style="page-break-before:always; margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><B><U>REPORTED RESULTS, BALANCE SHEET, PENSIONS &amp; &nbsp;OPEB </U></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Reported results</B>: during the fourth quarter, the deterioration of the economic environment and the drop in the company&#146;s market capitalisation led to a thorough re-assessment of the carrying value of goodwill and other intangible assets on its balance sheet. The re-assessment of the company&#146;s near-term outlook taking into account more conservative growth assumptions for the telecommunications equipment and related services market in 2009, coupled with the decision to streamline the company&#146;s portfolio and with the use of a higher discount rate led to an asset impairment charge of Euro (3.910) billion. This is reflected in the fourth quarter net loss (group share) of Euro&nbsp;(3.892)&nbsp;billion or Euro (1.72) per diluted share (USD (2.39) per ADS). For full year 2008, Alcatel-Lucent booked total asset impairment charges of Euro (4.725) billion, reflected in the full year net loss (group sha
re) of Euro (5.215) billion or Euro (2.31) per share (USD (3.22) per ADS).</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B><BR></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Balance sheet highlights</B>: the net (debt) cash position was Euro (389) million as of December 31, 2008, compared with Euro (600) million as of September 30, 2008. The sequential reduction in net debt of Euro 211 million primarily reflects the higher level of operating profitability this quarter and a positive contribution from Pensions and OPEB due to Section 420 transfers (Euro +99 million), partially offset by cash outflow related to restructuring (Euro (172) million). &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>

<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>In addition to the reported results, Alcatel-Lucent is providing adjusted results in order to provide meaningful comparable information, which exclude the main non-cash impacts from Purchase Price Allocation (PPA) entries in relation to the Lucent business combination. For the fourth quarter 2008, the adjusted<SUP style=font-size:7pt>2</SUP> gross profit was Euro 1.648 billion or 33.3% of revenue and the adjusted<SUP style=font-size:7pt>2</SUP> operating income<SUP style=font-size:7pt>1</SUP> was Euro&nbsp;297 million or 6.0% of revenues. </P>

Two of these three items impacted the adjusted gross profit with a net negative effect of Euro 14 million or 0.5<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>point in percentage of revenue. <P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B>CARRIER OPERATING SEGMENT</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:4.95pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>For the fourth quarter 2008, revenues for the Carrier operating segment were Euro 3.295<B> </B>billion a decrease of (11.8) % compared to Euro 3.734 billion in the year-ago quarter, and an increase of 20.5% compared to Euro 2.734 billion in the third quarter 2008. At constant currency exchange rates, Carrier revenues decreased 13.9%<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>year-over-year and increased<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>16.1%<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>sequentially. The segment posted an adjusted<SUP style=font-size:7pt>2</SUP> operating<SUP style=font-size:7pt>1</SUP> profit of Euro 45 million or an operating margin of 1.4% compared to a profit of Euro 93 million or a margin of 2.5% in the year ago period. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><B>Key highlights:</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Fixed access revenue declined at a double-digit rate this quarter, impacted by the ongoing decline of the legacy ADSL market in Europe and North America. Alcatel-Lucent shipped 6.7 million DSL ports in the quarter, down 21% from the year-ago quarter and up 12% sequentially. While the deployment of next generation, fibre-based access networks has been slower than expected for both regulatory and economic reasons, Alcatel-Lucent is best positioned to benefit, with a four-quarter rolling market share ending in the third quarter 2008 of 46% in both the<FONT COLOR=#FF0000> </FONT>VDSL and GPON segments, according to Dell&#146;Oro. &nbsp;&nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Alcatel-Lucent enjoyed its highest quarter ever in IP/MPLS service router revenues, with strong double-digit growth, reflecting the success of the upgraded product portfolio as well as increased customer diversification, notably in North America. The company&#146;s IP/MPLS service routers started shipping to 15 new customers in the fourth quarter, taking the total customer base to more than 250 customers in more than 100 countries. The ATM switching business continued on its structural decline path. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Optical networking revenue was impacted by the slowdown in the terrestrial market, particularly in the long distance Dense-Wave Division Multiplexing (D-WDM) segment. This was partly offset by very strong growth in submarine networks and healthy growth in microwave transmission. Alcatel-Lucent is enjoying good traction with its latest introductions, with 60 customers for the next generation optical transport system (1850 TSS) and a total of 25 contracts and trials for the newly introduced microwave packet transport system (9500 MPR). </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>In mobile networks, GSM revenue declined at a lower rate than in the third quarter as the Chinese market recovered after the freeze on network expansions due to the Olympics. W-CDMA revenue was more or less stable when compared to a strong year-ago quarter but grew approximately 50% for the year as whole. CDMA revenue declined at a much lower rate than in the prior two quarters and enjoyed strong sequential recovery, driven by shipments to a new customer in China as well as higher-than-expected capital spending by a large North American CDMA operator. &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B><BR>
<BR></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:18pt; font-family:Arial" align=right>7</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:12pt"><BR></P>
<HR style="padding-top:7.2pt; padding-bottom:7.2pt" noshade size=1.333>
<P style="page-break-before:always; margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>In the Chinese 3G market, where the vendor selection process is ongoing, Alcatel-Lucent believes it is now well positioned to be one of the top three suppliers and expects to be present in all three technologies (CDMA EV-DO, TD-SCDMA, and W-CDMA) with an increased market share compared to 2G technologies. This should result in material volume shipments starting this year, more than compensating for the expected decline of the Chinese 2G market. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>The company&#146;s core switching activities were impacted by the strong decline in legacy TDM voice. After another quarter of double-digit growth, revenues in NGN/IMS are now higher than in legacy TDM switching. Alcatel-Lucent&#146;s IMS solution continued to gain traction, with two more customer wins in Canada and Brazil this quarter.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; color:#FF0000" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; line-height:14pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:10pt" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:10pt" align=justify><B></B>Revenue from applications was impacted in the fourth quarter by the decline in legacy payment and Intelligent Networks (IN). Alcatel-Lucent nonetheless enjoyed good growth in Subscriber Data Management and Messaging. &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B><BR></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B>ENTERPRISE OPERATING SEGMENT</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:4.95pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>For the Fourth quarter 2008, revenues for the Enterprise operating segment were Euro 433 million, a decrease of (0.4)% compared to Euro 435 million in the year-ago quarter and an increase of 11.7% compared to Euro 388 million in the third quarter 2008. At constant currency exchange rates, Enterprise revenues decreased 2.4% year-over-year and increased 7.5% sequentially. Adjusted<SUP style=font-size:7pt>2</SUP> operating income<SUP style=font-size:7pt>1</SUP> was Euro 55 million, or 12.6% of revenues compared to Euro 56 million or 12.8% in the year-ago quarter. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>Key highlights: </B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Enterprise Solutions grew slightly in the quarter in spite of deteriorating economic conditions. The decline in the SMB voice telephony market was more than compensated by slight growth in the large enterprise voice telephony market and double-digit growth in data networking. Revenue from security solutions doubled in the quarter, albeit from a small base, driven in part by the success of the company&#146;s firewall solutions. &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Genesys, the contact centre software activity, returned to double-digit growth in the fourth quarter, with a stronger-than-expected finish to the year across all product lines and geographies. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>From a geographic standpoint, revenue grew strongly in North America driven by the ongoing implementation of a large end-to-end contract, contracted slightly in EMEA and declined in both Latin America and APAC. &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>The adjusted operating margin was almost stable from the year-ago quarter, in spite of the slight revenue decline, reflecting a slight improvement in the segment gross margin.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>

<BR></B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:18pt; font-family:Arial" align=right>8</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:12pt"><BR></P>
<HR style="padding-top:7.2pt; padding-bottom:7.2pt" noshade size=1.333>
<P style="page-break-before:always; margin:0pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt"><B>SERVICES OPERATING SEGMENT</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:4.95pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify>For the fourth quarter 2008, revenues for the Services operating segment were Euro 1.086<B> </B>billion an increase of 6.4% compared to Euro 1.020 billion in the year-ago quarter and an increase of 24.8% compared to Euro 870 million in the third quarter 2008. At constant currency exchange rates, Services revenues increased 6.6% year-over-year and increased 23.3% sequentially. Adjusted<SUP style=font-size:7pt>2</SUP> operating income<SUP style=font-size:7pt>1</SUP> was Euro 91 million or 8.4% of revenues compared to Euro 107 million or 10.5% of revenues in the year ago quarter.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:18.5pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><B>Key highlights: </B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial; font-size:11pt" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Network operations enjoyed another quarter of very strong growth, driven by some of the large contract wins booked throughout the year, including Brazil Telecom, Reliance, Orange Switzerland, Sunrise and BT. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Network integration reported growth in the mid teens this quarter, similar to the prior quarter, driven by complex network design, network optimisation and network transformation projects. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Professional services revenues were impacted by the above mentioned slow-down in the market for legacy applications including payment and IN. IPTV and OSS integration continued to grow strongly.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>Finally, Maintenance revenue grew at a mid single-digit rate this quarter. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:-0.25pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:-15pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; text-indent:-18pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B>&#149;</B></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:17.75pt; padding-right:-4.05pt; line-height:17pt; font-family:Arial" align=justify><B></B>The profitability of the segment was down both year-over-year and sequentially, which is largely attributable to a material provision for a turn-key contract. For the year 2008 as a whole, the adjusted operating margin of the Services segment improved materially from 4.6% to 7.8% which reflects the double-digit revenue growth, a favourable product mix and an improvement in the gross margin of all four activities.</P>
