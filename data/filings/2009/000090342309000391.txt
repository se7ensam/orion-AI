<p style=' margin-bottom:3pt; margin-top:3pt;text-align:center;'><u><b><font face="Arial" size=4>Abbey profits up over 25%</font></b></u></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:3pt; margin-top:3pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:3pt; margin-top:3pt;text-align:justify;'><u><b><font face="Arial" size=2>London, 29<sup>th</sup> April 2009</font></b></u></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:3pt; margin-top:3pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>This statement provides a summary of the unaudited business and financial trends for the three months to 31<sup>st</sup> March 2009. Unless otherwise stated, reference to Abbey and other general statements refer to the trading<sup>1 </sup>results and business flow analysis of the combined businesses of both Abbey (including B&amp;B<sup>2</sup>) and A&amp;L (transferred to Abbey in January 2009) compared to the trading results of Abbey and the pre-acquisition trading results of A&amp;L for the same period in 2008. </font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:3pt; margin-top:3pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>The first quarter 2009 results of Santander are also released today and can be found on </font><font color="#0000FF"><u><font face="Arial" size=2>www.santander.com</font></u></font><font face="Arial" size=2>. Abbey&#146;s results, on a Group basis, are included within Santander&#146;s financial statements.</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:3pt; margin-top:3pt;text-align:justify;'><b><font face="Arial" size=2>Chief Executive&#146;s Update</font></b></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:2pt; margin-top:12pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&#147;We have made a very strong start to 2009 with significant growth in profit and revenues. This is an excellent performance delivered in the toughest of economic environments.   </font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:2pt; margin-top:12pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>We have seen a good performance from all areas as we have continued to offer innovative value-for-money products, delivering a strong uplift in new business. We have increased mortgage and SME lending demonstrating our ongoing support for the UK economy. This growth is balanced against controlled costs and means that our cost to income ratio has reduced further to c.42%, as we continue to improve our efficiency in comparison to our competitors and toward our goal of being best in class.</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>Our prudent approach to our mortgage business continues to serve us well. Our overall mortgage book has a low loan-to-value of 52% and our new business lending continues to be based on affordability, robust risk management and a focus on credit quality. Our first quarter lending growth was also balanced by the growth in deposits, demonstrating that we continue to be seen as a safe and attractive home for UK deposits.</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>We are making good progress in integrating Bradford &amp; Bingley and Alliance &amp; Leicester with Abbey and we are fully on track to deliver the &pound;180m of cost synergies by 2011. </font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>I am prudently optimistic about our business prospects for the remainder of the year, and we continue to benefit from the strength and backing of Santander.&#148;</font></p>

  <u><b><font face="Arial" size=2>Financial results</font></b></u></p>
<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:3pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>Abbey statutory profit before tax from continuing operations up by more than 25% versus Q1 &#146;08. </font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:3pt;text-align:justify;'><b><font face="Arial" size=2>Trading income: </font></b></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>Net interest income was significantly ahead compared to last year and largely driven by an excellent performance in Retail Banking, benefiting from improved mortgage new business and retention margins throughout 2008 and a continuation of this trend into the first quarter of 2009. Corporate Banking performance was ahead, driven by prudent lending growth and strong deposit acquisition during 2008 and continuing into 2009. Private Banking results were broadly in line with last year as customer deposits continue to grow, up over 10%, through the Cater Allen and Abbey International businesses. </font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>Non-interest income was broadly in line with last year, driven largely by an excellent first quarter by Global Banking and Markets. Retail Banking net fee income was lower than Q1 &#146;08, impacted by lower fees from current accounts and a reduction in mortgages redemption related income, general insurance and unsecured lending. Corporate Banking performance was impacted by a fall in A&amp;L branded net fees as a result of lower volumes in the money transmission business. Private Banking was broadly in line with last year. </font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:3pt;text-align:justify;'><b><font face="Arial" size=2>Trading expenses:</font></b></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>Trading expenses increased largely due to the inclusion of the B&amp;B related costs following the acquisition last year. Excluding the impact of B&amp;B, costs declined by c. 3%.</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:3pt;text-align:justify;'><b><font face="Arial" size=2>Trading provisions: </font></b></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>As anticipated, the provisions charge is significantly higher than the same period in 2008, albeit in-line with the final quarter of 2008, largely due to the mortgage portfolio. In preparation of the worsening economic environment, Abbey tightened credit criteria during Q4 &#145;07 and has continued tightening during 2008 and into Q1 &#146;09. In addition, following the transfer of A&amp;L to Abbey, the credit policies of the two businesses have been aligned. A conservative level of coverage has been maintained across these two businesses and this, together with the high quality prime residential nature of the portfolio and reduced exposure to unsecured lending, puts Abbey in a robust position given the economic environment.</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>Collection activities continue to perform well and in line with expectations, with the Q1&#146;09 level of properties in possession less than one third of the industry benchmark. Collection policies include a number of initiatives to help customers facing financial difficulty, including a range of solutions to reflect an individual's change in financial or personal circumstances and providing help to obtain independent third-party financial advice. </font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>The stock of properties in possession has increased by only 79 properties from the year end position of 969, reflecting the current difficult market conditions (properties in possession represent only 0.07% of the overall portfolio). In terms of 3 month plus arrears, the increase to 1.13% from the year end position of 0.93% is lower than expected as the low interest rate environment provides an additional element of assistance to financially stretched customers. Both the Abbey and Alliance &amp; Leicester brands remain significantly better than the industry based on the most recent CML figures for arrears. Equally, both brands consist of prime lending stock, with conservative LTV distribution. </font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>Properties in possession in the commercial mortgages portfolio increased by only 7 properties from the year end position of 24 and represent only 0.2% of assets.</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style=' margin-bottom:0pt; margin-top:0pt;text-align:justify;'><font face="Arial" size=2>Credit quality remains strong, with the average loan to value (LTV) on new business completions in Q1 &#146;09 slightly lower at 59% (Q4 &#145;08: 60%) and stock increasing slightly to 52% (Q4 &#145;08: 51%) driven by declining house prices.</font></p>
