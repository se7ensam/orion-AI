<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Commenting on the results, Rick Yan, President and Chief Executive Officer of 51job, Inc., said,
&#147;Our aggressive cost and expense management and focus on business process efficiency improvements
yielded tangible results in the second quarter. We successfully drove margin expansion and
delivered solid earnings despite challenging market conditions which continued to impact customer
demand and revenues. Although spending per customer declined, we were pleased to see regained
momentum in new customer acquisitions for our online business in the second quarter. The decisions
and progress we have made to strengthen our operations and enhance our resource allocation this
year reinforce our commitment to achieve sustainable growth and profitability for our shareholders
over the long term.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt"><B>Second Quarter 2009 Unaudited Financial Results</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Total revenues for the second quarter ended June&nbsp;30, 2009 were RMB193.3&nbsp;million (US$28.3&nbsp;million),
a decrease of 11.6% from RMB218.7&nbsp;million for the same quarter in 2008.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Print advertising revenues for the second quarter of 2009 decreased 28.5% to RMB67.2&nbsp;million
(US$9.8&nbsp;million) compared with RMB93.9&nbsp;million for the same quarter in 2008. The decrease was
primarily due to a lower volume of print advertising pages in <I>51job Weekly, </I>which was affected by a
decline in market demand resulting from the continued impact of the global economic downturn and
the slower economic growth environment in China. The estimated number of print advertising pages
generated in the second quarter of 2009 decreased 32.9% to 2,860 compared with 4,263 pages in the
same quarter in 2008. Although print advertising prices charged in each city remained relatively
stable as compared to the second quarter of 2008, overall average revenue per page increased 6.6%
due to an increase in page volume contribution from cities where print advertising prices are
generally higher as compared to the same quarter of the prior year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Online recruitment services revenues for the second quarter of 2009 were RMB77.4&nbsp;million (US$11.3
million), representing a 7.1% decrease from RMB83.3&nbsp;million for the same quarter of the prior year.
The decrease primarily resulted from lower average revenue per unique employer, which was
partially offset by an increase in the number of unique employers using the Company&#146;s online
recruitment services. Average revenue per unique employer decreased 23.9% in the second quarter of
2009 as compared to the same quarter in 2008 due to employers purchasing lower priced products
and/or reducing their overall spending on online recruitment services. Unique employers using the
Company&#146;s online recruitment services increased 22.1% to 79,151 in the second quarter of 2009
compared with 64,813 in the same quarter of the prior year driven by an acceleration in customer
adoption of online recruitment services.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Other human resource related revenues for the second quarter of 2009 increased 17.4% to RMB48.7
million (US$7.1&nbsp;million) from RMB41.5&nbsp;million in the same quarter of 2008 principally due to
greater customer demand for human resource outsourcing services, which was partially offset by
lower revenues from executive search and training services.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Gross profit for the second quarter of 2009 decreased 4.2% to RMB109.6&nbsp;million (US$16.0&nbsp;million)
from RMB114.4&nbsp;million for the same quarter of the prior year. Gross margin, which is equal to
gross profit divided by net revenues, improved to 59.8% in the second quarter of 2009 compared with
54.9% in the same quarter in 2008 primarily due to lower printing related and other direct expenses
resulting from the implementation of cost control and efficiency measures.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Operating expenses for the second quarter of 2009 were RMB82.8&nbsp;million (US$12.1&nbsp;million) compared
with RMB84.1&nbsp;million for the same quarter of 2008. Operating expenses as a percentage of net
revenues was 45.2% for the second quarter of 2009 compared with 40.4% for the second quarter of
2008. Excluding share-based compensation expense, operating expenses as a percentage of net
revenues was 42.1% in the second quarter of 2009 compared with 37.5% in the second quarter of 2008.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Sales and marketing expenses for the second quarter of 2009 decreased 7.1% to RMB48.5&nbsp;million
(US$7.1&nbsp;million) from RMB52.2&nbsp;million for the same quarter of the prior year primarily due to a
decrease in advertising and promotion expenses and travel and entertainment expenses, which were
partially offset by higher salary and benefits expenses compared to the same quarter in 2008.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">General and administrative expenses for the second quarter of 2009 increased 7.4% to RMB34.3
million (US$5.0&nbsp;million) from RMB31.9&nbsp;million in the second quarter of 2008, which was mainly
attributable to higher rental, depreciation and employee related expenses incurred in the second
quarter of 2009.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Income from operations for the second quarter of 2009 was RMB26.8&nbsp;million (US$3.9&nbsp;million) compared
with RMB30.3&nbsp;million for the same quarter of the prior year. The Company&#146;s effective tax rate was
32.7% in the second quarter of 2009 compared with 36.3% in the second quarter of the prior year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Net income for the second quarter of 2009 was RMB20.8&nbsp;million (US$3.0&nbsp;million) compared with
RMB19.3&nbsp;million for the same quarter in 2008. Fully diluted earnings per common share for the
second quarter of 2009 were RMB0.37 (US$0.05) compared with RMB0.34 for the same quarter in 2008.
Fully diluted earnings per ADS for the second quarter of 2009 were RMB0.74 (US$0.11) compared with
RMB0.68 in the second quarter of 2008.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">In the second quarter of 2009, the Company recognized total share-based compensation expense of
RMB6.8&nbsp;million (US$1.0&nbsp;million) compared with RMB7.2&nbsp;million in the second quarter of 2008. The
Company also recognized a foreign currency translation loss of RMB142,000 (US$21,000) in the second
quarter of 2009 compared with a translation loss of RMB6.6&nbsp;million in the second quarter of 2008.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Excluding share-based compensation expense and the impact of foreign currency translation loss,
non-GAAP adjusted income for the second quarter of 2009 was RMB27.7&nbsp;million (US$4.1&nbsp;million)
compared with RMB33.1&nbsp;million for the second quarter of 2008. Non-GAAP adjusted fully diluted
earnings per common share were RMB0.50 (US$0.07) in the second quarter of 2009 compared with
RMB0.58 in the second quarter of 2008. Non-GAAP adjusted fully diluted earnings per ADS in the
second quarter of 2009 were RMB0.99 (US$0.14) compared with RMB1.17 in the second quarter of 2008.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt"><B>Six Months 2009 Unaudited Financial Results</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Total revenues for the six months ended June&nbsp;30, 2009 were RMB371.6&nbsp;million (US$54.4&nbsp;million), a
decrease of 18.4% from RMB455.2&nbsp;million in the comparable period in 2008. Income from operations
for the six months ended June&nbsp;30, 2009 decreased to RMB38.5&nbsp;million (US$5.6&nbsp;million) from RMB68.8
million for the same period last year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Net income for the first half of 2009 decreased to RMB30.2&nbsp;million (US$4.4&nbsp;million) from RMB41.6
million for the same period in 2008. Fully diluted earnings per common share for the first half of
2009 decreased to RMB0.54 (US$0.08) from RMB0.73 in the comparable period in 2008. Fully diluted
earnings per ADS for the first half of 2009 were RMB1.08 (US$0.16) compared with RMB1.47 in the
same period in 2008.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Excluding share-based compensation and foreign currency translation loss, non-GAAP adjusted net
income for the six months ended June&nbsp;30, 2009 decreased to RMB44.7&nbsp;million (US$6.5&nbsp;million) from
RMB71.9&nbsp;million for the six months ended June&nbsp;30, 2008. Non-GAAP adjusted fully diluted earnings
per common share were RMB0.80 (US$0.12) in first half of 2009 compared with RMB1.27 in the same
period in 2008. Non-GAAP adjusted fully diluted earnings per ADS in the first half of 2009 were
RMB1.59 (US$0.23) compared with RMB2.53 in the same period in 2008.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">In September&nbsp;2008, the Company announced that its shareholders had approved a share repurchase
program authorizing the repurchase of up to US$25&nbsp;million worth of outstanding ADSs. In the second
quarter of 2009, the Company repurchased 148,195 ADSs, representing 296,390 common shares, in the
open market for an aggregate consideration of US$1.2&nbsp;million, including transaction fees. Since
the inception of this share repurchase program, the Company has repurchased a total of 521,042
ADSs, representing 1,042,084 common shares, for an aggregate consideration of US$3.8&nbsp;million.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">As of June&nbsp;30, 2009, the Company had cash and short-term investments totaling RMB1,113.1&nbsp;million
(US$163.0&nbsp;million) compared with RMB1,074.4&nbsp;million at December&nbsp;31, 2008. Short-term investments
consist of certificates of deposit held by the Company in banking institutions in China.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt"><B>Business Outlook</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">For the third quarter of 2009, based on current market and operating conditions, the Company&#146;s

loss or gain, the Company&#146;s non-GAAP fully diluted earnings target for the third quarter of 2009 is

the estimated range of RMB6&nbsp;million to RMB7&nbsp;million (US$0.9&nbsp;million to US$1.0&nbsp;million).

<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt"><B>Use of Non-GAAP Financial Measures</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">To supplement the consolidated financial statements presented in accordance with United States
Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), 51job uses non-GAAP measures of adjusted net
income, adjusted earnings per common share and adjusted earnings per ADS, which are adjusted from
results based on GAAP to exclude the impact of share-based compensation expense and foreign
currency translation gain or loss. The Company believes excluding share-based compensation expense
from its non-GAAP financial measures is useful for its management and investors to assess and
analyze the Company&#146;s core operating results as such expense is not directly attributable to the
underlying performance of the Company&#146;s business operations and do not impact its cash earnings.
The Company believes excluding foreign currency translation gain or loss from its non-GAAP
financial measures is useful for its management and investors as such translation gain or loss is
unrelated to the Company&#146;s core business operations and will not result in cash settlement nor
impact the Company&#146;s cash earnings. 51job also believes these non-GAAP measures excluding
share-based compensation expense and foreign currency translation gain or loss are important in
helping investors to understand the Company&#146;s current financial performance and future prospects
and to compare business trends among different reporting periods on a consistent basis. The
presentation of these additional measures should not be considered a substitute for or superior to
GAAP results or as being comparable to results reported or forecasted by other companies. The
non-GAAP measures have been reconciled to GAAP measures in the attached financial statements.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt"><B>About 51job</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">51job, Inc. (Nasdaq: JOBS) is a leading provider of integrated human resource services in China
with a strong focus on recruitment related services. Offering a broad array of products and
services, 51job connects millions of job seekers with employment opportunities and streamlines the
recruitment process and human resource administration for tens of thousands of companies in China.
Through print advertisements in <I>51job Weekly </I>and online recruitment services at <I>www.51job.com</I>, both
domestic Chinese employers and multinational companies alike are able to attract, identify and
recruit new employees. 51job also provides a number of other human resource services, including
executive search, training, business process outsourcing and salary surveys. 51job&#146;s nationwide
office network in China spans 26 cities operating 22 local editions of <I>51job Weekly</I>.
